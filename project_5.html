I Fought the Law: Predicting Outcomes in Virginia's Courts

In 2017, Virginia's District Criminal and Traffic Courts processed over 2 million hearings for violations ranging from speeding tickets to serious felonies. For those facing charges from the State of Virginia, there is no data online to support whether, and how, to contest charges to improve the odds of winning a case or minimizing penalties.

With information scraped from the Virginia Courts website, I used topic modeling, classification models, and ANOVA tests to predict the outcomes of cases based on defense strategy and defendant circumstances and analyze potential racial bias in the system.

Tools & Techniques Used

  • Topic Modeling: NLTK, Scikit-learn (LDA)
  • Classification Modeling: Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest
  • Statistical Tests: StatsModels (ANOVA)
  • Visualizations: Seaborn, Plotly
