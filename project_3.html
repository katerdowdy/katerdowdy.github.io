SAT vs. ACT: Battle for Statewide Testing

The majority of four-year colleges and universities across the U.S. require prospective students to submit scores from either the SAT or ACT as part of their application. For this class project, I was asked to provide a data-backed recommendation to the College Board on which states to focus efforts and resources to drive increased participation in the SAT (vs. the ACT).

Using outside research and just two years of state aggregate participation and performance metrics on both tests, I crafted a narrative around statewide testing requirements and rankings to build a case for the College Board.

Tools & Techniques Used

  • Data Exploration: Python, Pandas
  • Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn
